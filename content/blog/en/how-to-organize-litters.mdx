## The problem: scattered information

Most kennels track litter planning in at least three different places: an Excel sheet with predicted dates, WhatsApp with the external stud's owner, and a wall calendar with vet visits. When delivery day comes, that fragmentation shows.

## The milestones that cannot fail

Every well-managed litter has five critical moments that **must** be logged in one place:

1. **Mating**: exact day and time, with identified male.
2. **Confirmed ovulation**: progesterone, ovulation date.
3. **Ultrasound**: pregnancy confirmation at 25-30 days.
4. **Whelping**: timestamp per puppy and birth weight.
5. **Weaning**: 8 weeks (breed-dependent), with prior weekly weighing.

If two of these milestones slip, your per-dam fertility reports lose value, and with them your ability to decide when to retire a breeder.

## How Petwellly does it

In Petwellly each litter is an object that **hooks** into everything else:

- When you confirm mating, ultrasound (day 28) and forecasted whelping (day 63) are auto-scheduled.
- When you log the whelping, individual puppy records are created with birth date and initial weight.
- Pre-natal reservations are linked to the puppy assigned at weaning.
- If you flagged the male as "external stud", their pedigree and the owner's contact stay linked to the litter.

## Checklist to start

> You don't need to do everything at once. These are the things you **should** put in your system this week:

- [ ] List of your active breeders with their pedigree.
- [ ] Next predicted heats (next 2 months).
- [ ] Default vet assigned per dam.
- [ ] Sale contract template ready to use.
- [ ] Waiting list of interested clients.

With those five points, your next litter will look like it came from a different kennel.

## The fertility report

Six months in with the data clean, the per-dam fertility report becomes operational: it tells you which dams are giving small litters, which have longer cycle intervals and which have higher neonatal survival. Three data points enough to make hard decisions with criteria.
