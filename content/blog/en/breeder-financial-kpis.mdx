## Why almost nobody tracks them?

Because doing the math by hand is hell. To know the real margin of a litter you must add food, vaccines, microchips, registrations, vet visits, transport and a share of fixed costs (electricity, insurance, depreciation), and subtract from revenue. If that calculation takes more than 15 minutes, you won't do it.

## The 5 essentials

### 1. Margin per litter

Litter revenue minus directly-attributable costs (food, vet, registrations, transport). **Not** gross revenue.

### 2. Average cost per puppy

Total litter cost divided by *delivered* puppies (not born). This penalizes neonatal mortality in the calculation, which is correct.

### 3. Average time to sale

Days between weaning and delivery. Measures the health of your sales pipeline. If it exceeds 30 days, you have a lead-gen problem.

### 4. Genetic investment ROI

If you buy a 4,000€ dam for breeding, in how many litters do you amortize? Petwellly distributes cost across delivered litters and gives you the recovered percentage.

### 5. Customer lifetime value

How much a client has paid you across all purchases and services. Recurring clients are 3-4× more valuable than new ones; tracking this tells you where to focus commercial effort.

## What you should NOT measure

Beware vanity metrics. Instagram followers, web visits or Facebook likes are not financial KPIs. They might indicate awareness, but they won't make decisions for you.
