## Why delivery is the most critical moment

You've spent 8 weeks raising a puppy. Delivery takes 30 minutes. If you forget the health booklet in those 30 minutes, the client leaves with a sour taste even if the animal is perfect. And a sour taste costs a negative Google review.

## The 12-item checklist

### Documentation (6 items)

1. **Health booklet** complete, signed and stamped.
2. **Pedigree**, original or provisional certificate per breed.
3. **Sale contract** signed by both parties.
4. **Microchip certificate** registered to the buyer.
5. **Health warranty** per applicable regulation.
6. **Genetic tests** of the parents in PDF or print.

### Materials (3 items)

7. **Welcome kit**: food for the next days, a toy with the dam's smell, a used blanket.
8. **Feeding plan** detailed by age.
9. **Vaccination plan** with upcoming doses.

### Operations (3 items)

10. **Final payment** collected and reconciled.
11. **Delivery photo** (with client consent, for the portal and potential reviews).
12. **2-week follow-up appointment** scheduled in the client portal.

## How Petwellly does it

Each reservation has an embedded checklist that **blocks** the "delivered" mark until everything is OK. If the contract isn't signed, you can't close the delivery.

Also, when closing delivery:

- The client gets an automatic email with all documents in PDF and links to the portal.
- The first post-delivery appointment is scheduled.
- An automatic review request is sent 14 days later.

## The most common mistake

Thinking that "if the puppy is fine, paperwork doesn't matter". It doesn't matter until the day a client wants to file a complaint and the contract wasn't signed. That day you realize the value of the checklist.

> **Mini test:** can you, right now, locate the health booklet of your last delivered puppy? If it takes more than 30 seconds, you have a document-organization problem. The fix is to centralize in a single system.
